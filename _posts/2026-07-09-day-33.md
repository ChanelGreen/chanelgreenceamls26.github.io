what_i_learned: |
            Today I began exploring cost-sensitive learning as an alternative to SMOTE for handling class imbalance in machine learning. I learned that, unlike SMOTE, cost-sensitive learning does not create synthetic data. Instead, it adjusts the learning process by assigning greater importance to the minority class, encouraging the model to pay closer attention to those observations during training. I also learned why researchers use techniques like Stratified Cross-Validation and alternative imbalance methods to ensure that improvements in model performance are reliable, generalizable, and not dependent on a single train-test split.
