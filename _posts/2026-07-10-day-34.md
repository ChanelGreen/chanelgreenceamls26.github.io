what_i_learned: |
Today I shifted my focus from coding to research writing by translating my completed Jupyter Notebook into my Overleaf research paper. I learned that writing a research paper is more than copying code and results—it requires organizing the work into a logical story that explains the problem, methodology, experiments, and findings. I also gained a better understanding of how each section of the paper connects directly to my research questions, ensuring that every experiment has a clear purpose and supports the overall objectives of the study.
blockers:   | 
I did not encounter any major blockers today. My primary focus was staying organized while transferring my notebook into a formal research paper and making sure each section flowed naturally from one to the next.
reflection: |
Today was a reminder that research doesn't end when the models finish running. Communicating the work clearly is just as important as building the models themselves. As I converted my notebook into a structured paper, I found myself thinking more critically about why each experiment was performed and how it contributed to answering my research questions. Seeing the methodology and results come together in a professional research paper has been rewarding, and it reinforced how much my understanding of both machine learning and scientific writing has grown throughout this internship. I'm excited to continue building the Results section and watch the entire project come together.
