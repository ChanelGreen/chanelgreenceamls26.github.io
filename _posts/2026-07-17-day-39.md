what_i_learned: |
                One of the biggest things I learned today is that finishing the experiments is only part of the research process. Just as important is making sure the results are presented clearly and flow well. I spent most of the day reviewing my Overleaf report, tightening up the writing, removing repetition, and making sure the methodology, results, and discussion all connected smoothly.
                I also realized how much one new experiment can impact the rest of a paper. Adding the Douglas-Rachford optimization meant updating tables, figures, comparisons, and conclusions to keep everything consistent. It reinforced that a strong research paper isn't just about good results—it's about presenting those results in a clear, organized, and cohesive way.
