what_i_learned: |
      Today started with an Interview and Data Analysis 101 session led by Dr. Dacon. During the session, I learned more about how data can be used to answer questions, identify patterns, and support research findings. We discussed the importance of understanding not just the data itself, but also the story the data is telling and how to communicate those findings to others.
      After lunch, my project team continued diving deeper into our research project. We separated our work into specific focus areas, including Asthma, Cancer, Diabetes, and Chronic Health Disease datasets. My focus remained on the Diabetes dataset, where I continued exploring the features, understanding the purpose of the variables, and learning how environmental, demographic, socioeconomic, and health-related factors may contribute to diabetes burden within Baltimore communities. The more I work with the data, the more I understand that research is not just about coding—it's about asking questions and finding meaningful answers.
blockers: |  
      My biggest challenge today was not technical. After spending four days at the American Association of Family and Consumer Sciences Conference in Kentucky, I was still mentally transitioning back into research mode. I was grateful to spend time with the students, graduate mentors, and faculty today, and I enjoyed the collaborative environment. However, I noticed a shift in the atmosphere once administration entered the space, and things felt a little more confined and tense than they had earlier in the day.
      Rather than allowing that feeling to distract me, I chose to work through it by remaining focused, staying quiet when necessary, and concentrating on the task in front of me. By the end of the day, I was able to refocus my energy on the project and continue making progress.
reflection: |
      Today's experience reminded me that growth happens in different ways. Some days growth comes through learning new technical skills, and other days it comes through learning how to adapt to changing environments and remain focused despite distractions. I enjoyed continuing to explore the diabetes dataset and gaining a better understanding of how community factors connect to health outcomes. As the project becomes more specialized, I am starting to feel more confident in my ability to explain the data, understand the research process, and contribute meaningful insights to my team's work. Each day I become a little more comfortable with both the technical and analytical side of research.
