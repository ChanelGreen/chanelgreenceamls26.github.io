what_i_learned: |
              Today our team presented our findings from the data exploration and preprocessing task list. It was rewarding to see how each of our individual                       projects    —Asthma, Cancer, Coronary Heart Disease, and Diabetes—fit together under the larger goal of predicting respiratory health risks using                     machine learning. We also explored different methods for balancing imbalanced datasets, including Random Oversampling, Random Undersampling, ADASYN,                 and SMOTE. I learned that each technique has its own strengths and trade-offs, and that choosing the right method depends on the characteristics of the               dataset and the goals of the machine learning model. It reinforced that preparing quality data is just as important as building the model itself.
blockers:   | 
            My biggest challenge today wasn't completing the work—it was communicating what I learned. As someone whose background isn't in mathematics or computer               science, shifting into a mindset focused on datasets, statistics, and machine learning concepts can be challenging. I understood the overall ideas, but               finding the right words to explain technical concepts with confidence took extra effort.
reflection: |
           Today reminded me that learning data science isn't just about writing code—it's also about developing the ability to communicate complex ideas clearly.               Every presentation pushes me a little further outside my comfort zone, and although I don't naturally think like a mathematician, I'm beginning to think              more like a researcher. I'm learning to ask better questions, interpret data more critically, and connect technical findings to real-world public health              challenges. Growth doesn't happen by already knowing the answers—it happens by continuing to learn, practice, and speak up even when you're still building            confidence.
