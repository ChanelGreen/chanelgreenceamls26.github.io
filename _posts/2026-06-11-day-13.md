what_i_learned: |
       Today, we reviewed and discussed the eight Python Data Analysis videos, focusing on the concepts, commands, and overall data analysis process. Talking through each lesson helped reinforce my understanding of data cleaning, text manipulation, preparing numeric data, working with dates, joining datasets, frequency tables, plotting with Pandas, and descriptive statistics. We also began exploring two new software programs that will support our research journey: RStudio and MATLAB. I learned that both tools are widely used for statistical analysis, data visualization, and research, and I gained a basic understanding of their purpose and functionality within data science and machine learning workflows.

blockers: |  
My biggest challenge today was becoming familiar with the RStudio environment. While some aspects of RStudio felt similar to Jupyter Notebook, I had difficulty following along with the tutorial and successfully importing the datasets provided. The dataset files did not appear to match the examples shown in the video, which made it difficult to reproduce the same results and fully understand the workflow. Navigating a new software environment while learning a new programming language added an extra layer of complexity to the learning process.

reflection: |
Today's experience reminded me that learning new tools takes patience. Although I felt more comfortable discussing the Python concepts because I had already spent time practicing them, stepping into RStudio and MATLAB pushed me outside of my comfort zone. Even though I did not master either software today, I gained exposure to the platforms and developed a better understanding of how they fit into the broader research process. I also realized that progress is not always measured by completing every task perfectly; sometimes progress comes from identifying what needs additional practice and giving yourself permission to learn at your own pace. As I continue this journey, I want to focus on building confidence with each tool one step at a time rather than trying to learn everything at once.
