what_i_learned: |
      Today, I continued working on our diabetes research project using the Environmental Justice Index (EJI) dataset. We focused on understanding the meaning of the features within the dataset and why they were selected for our analysis. This helped me move beyond simply running code and begin thinking more critically about the story the data is telling. I learned how environmental, demographic, socioeconomic, and health-related factors can be used to better understand diabetes burden across Baltimore communities.
      We also spent time exploring the dataset through histograms, boxplots, outlier analysis, and skewness analysis. These activities helped me better understand the distribution of the data and identify patterns that may influence our future machine learning models. One of the biggest takeaways from today was learning how to explain the dataset in plain language and connect the numbers back to real people and real communities.
blockers: |  
      My biggest challenge today was transitioning back into research mode after returning from the four-day American Association of Family and Consumer Sciences (AAFCS) Conference in Kentucky. The conference was an incredible experience filled with presentations, networking, and professional development opportunities, but it required me to shift gears mentally once I returned. Moving from conference learning and professional engagement back into coding, data analysis, and research took some adjustment. Once I settled back into the project, however, I was able to refocus and continue making progress.
reflection: |
       Today's work reminded me that data is more than numbers on a screen. Every variable in our dataset represents something happening within a community, whether it is access to healthcare, poverty levels, environmental exposures, or health behaviors. As someone studying Family and Consumer Sciences, I naturally think about how environments impact people and families, so I enjoyed connecting those ideas to our data analysis work. The more I learn, the more I see how research can be used to identify challenges, inform solutions, and ultimately improve the well-being of communities. I am beginning to feel more confident not only using the tools but also explaining the purpose behind the work we are doing.
