what_i_learned: |
      Today was our first day on the Morgan State University campus for the Summer AI Research Incubator, and it was exciting to finally transition from virtual learning to an in-person environment. We participated in a Data Analytics Workshop, where we explored how data can be collected, analyzed, and used to support decision-making and research. We also attended an AI Bias and Ethics Workshop, which highlighted the importance of fairness, transparency, and accountability when developing and using artificial intelligence systems. In addition to the workshops, we toured our lab spaces and learned more about the resources available to support our research throughout the summer. Collaborating with my peers and mentors in person created opportunities for discussion, idea-sharing, and deeper engagement with the material.
blockers: |  
      I did not experience any significant blockers today. The transition from virtual learning to the campus environment was smooth, and I was able to actively participate in the workshops and discussions. Having direct access to mentors, classmates, and research spaces helped create a supportive learning environment.
reflection: |
      Today felt like the beginning of a new phase in the research experience. While the virtual sessions provided a strong foundation, being on campus brought the work to life in a different way. Seeing the lab spaces, interacting with my cohort face-to-face, and engaging in conversations beyond a computer screen made the experience feel more real and collaborative. The AI Bias and Ethics workshop was especially impactful because it reinforced that technology is not just about building systems—it is also about understanding how those systems affect people and communities. I left today feeling energized, motivated, and excited for the opportunities ahead as we continue our research journey together.
