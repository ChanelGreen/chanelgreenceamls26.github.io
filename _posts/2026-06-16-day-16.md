what_i_learned: |
Today, I was introduced to LaTeX and Overleaf, two tools widely used in research, academia, and scientific publishing. We learned how to download and set up LaTeX, navigate the Overleaf platform, and create professional documents using LaTeX commands. Through the training videos, I learned how to structure documents with sections and subsections, format text, create lists, insert tables and images, and understand how Overleaf automatically compiles code into a polished document. I also learned that Overleaf allows multiple users to collaborate on the same document, making it a valuable tool for research teams and academic projects.
blockers: |  
I did not experience any major blockers today. The videos were easy to follow, and I enjoyed learning a new tool that directly connects to research writing and professional document preparation. As I practiced the commands and explored the platform, I became more comfortable understanding how LaTeX code translates into a finished document.
reflection: |
Today's lesson gave me a greater appreciation for the amount of work that goes into creating professional research papers and publications. While Overleaf and LaTeX initially looked intimidating because of the coding syntax, I quickly realized that the structure and organization make it easier to create consistent, polished documents. Learning this tool also helped me see how the different pieces of our summer research experience connect together—from collecting and analyzing data to communicating findings in a professional format. As I continue developing my research skills, I am excited to use Overleaf to document our work and contribute to future presentations, reports, and publications.
